## 3. Brand Guidelines

### Color Palette

The palette draws from the tradition of Renaissance oil painting — rich golds against deep, dramatic darks, with warm accents that evoke candlelit studios and gilded frames.

| Role | Color | Hex Code | Usage |
|------|-------|----------|-------|
| **Primary** | Atelier Black | `#0A0A0A` | Backgrounds, primary surfaces, feed aesthetic |
| **Secondary** | Pawcasso Gold | `#C8963E` | Logo, accents, highlight covers, key typography |
| **Accent 1** | Warm Ivory | `#F5E6CC` | Body text on dark backgrounds, subtle highlights |
| **Accent 2** | Deep Umber | `#3D2B1F` | Secondary backgrounds, gradient transitions |
| **Accent 3** | Renaissance Red | `#8B2500` | Sparingly — call-to-action buttons, sale announcements |

The feed should be **predominantly dark** with gold and warm tones emerging from the darkness, echoing the chiaroscuro lighting technique of Caravaggio and Rembrandt. Avoid bright whites, neons, or cool-toned colors that would break the gallery atmosphere.

---

### Typography

| Use Case | Recommended Font | Fallback | Notes |
|----------|-----------------|----------|-------|
| **Display / Titles** | Playfair Display | Georgia, serif | Use for post titles, quote graphics, and any large text overlays. The high-contrast serif evokes classical elegance. |
| **Body / Captions** | Lora | Times New Roman, serif | Use for longer caption text in graphics. Readable and warm. |
| **UI / Secondary** | Montserrat (Light or Regular) | Helvetica, sans-serif | Use for hashtags, small labels, website UI, and any functional text that needs clean legibility. |

All text in graphics should use **warm ivory (#F5E6CC)** or **gold (#C8963E)** on dark backgrounds. Avoid pure white (#FFFFFF), which feels too stark for this brand. When text must appear on lighter surfaces, use Atelier Black (#0A0A0A).

### Tone of Voice

The brand voice of Pawcasso Atelier should feel like a **refined but approachable art curator** — someone who deeply respects both the art and the animals, and speaks with quiet confidence rather than hype.

**Core Principles:**

The writing should be **warm and reverent**, treating each animal portrait as a genuine work of art rather than a novelty product. Captions should feel like gallery wall text — thoughtful, evocative, and concise. The tone avoids hard-sell language, internet slang, and excessive punctuation. Humor is welcome but should be dry and sophisticated rather than meme-driven.

**Do:**
- Use rich, sensory language ("luminous fur rendered in amber and ochre")
- Reference art history naturally ("a composition worthy of the Dutch Golden Age")
- Speak about animals with genuine warmth and dignity
- Keep captions concise — let the artwork speak

**Don't:**
- Use words like "cute," "adorable," or "fur baby" (too casual for the brand)
- Over-explain the process or mention AI/technology
- Use aggressive CTAs ("BUY NOW!", "Don't miss out!")
- Post without intention — every caption should add value

**Example Caption:**

> *A study in patience and golden light.*
>
> *Meet Oliver — a gentleman of distinguished whiskers and quiet authority, rendered here in the Flemish tradition. Oil-rich brushwork traces the arc of each ear; warm sienna deepens in the shadows beneath his chin.*
>
> *Commission your portrait via the link in bio.*
>
> *#AnimalPortrait #PetArt #FineArt #OilPainting #Pawcasso*

### Visual Style Rules

**Feed Aesthetic:**

The Instagram grid should feel like walking through a **dimly lit, high-end gallery**. Dark backgrounds dominate, with each portrait emerging from shadow into warm, golden light. When viewed as a grid, the feed should have a cohesive, almost monochromatic warmth — blacks, golds, ambers, and deep browns — punctuated occasionally by the rich colors within the portraits themselves.

**Composition Guidelines:**

Every post should follow one of three core formats to maintain grid consistency. The first is the **Portrait Showcase**, which features the finished artwork on a dark background, optionally with a subtle gilded frame effect, occupying roughly 70-80% of the frame. The second is the **Detail Close-Up**, a tight crop on brushwork, texture, or the animal's eyes to highlight craftsmanship. The third is the **Text Post**, which uses the brand typography on a dark background for quotes, announcements, or educational art history content.

**Posting Rules:**

Maintain a ratio of approximately 4:1 between artwork posts and text/promotional posts. Never post two text-heavy images back to back. Alternate between full portraits and detail crops to create visual rhythm in the grid. Every third row of the grid (when viewed as 3-column) should feel balanced in terms of light distribution — avoid clustering all the brightest portraits together.

**Stories & Reels:**

Stories should use the same dark aesthetic with gold text overlays. Process videos (time-lapses of portrait creation) are the highest-performing content format for art accounts and should be posted as Reels at least twice per week. Use the Warm Ivory (#F5E6CC) for any text overlays in Stories, and keep animations minimal and elegant — simple fades, no bouncing text or flashy transitions.
